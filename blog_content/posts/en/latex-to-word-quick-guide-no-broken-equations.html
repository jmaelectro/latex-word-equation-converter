Goal

Convert a full LaTeX document to Word without ending up with screenshots or broken equation text. Target output: native editable Word equations (OMML).

7-step quick checklist

  1. Use clean delimiters: $...$ for inline and $$...$$ or \[...\] for display math.
  2. Minimize custom macros if they are not strictly needed.
  3. Keep one style for delimiters across the document.
  4. Export source to .docx or .txt first.
  5. Convert to OMML using Equations to Word.
  6. Spot-check difficult formulas (integrals, roots, matrices, aligned blocks).
  7. Apply final formatting (headings, spacing, numbering) inside Word.

Sample input

Let $f(x)=x^2$.
In display form:
\[ \int_0^1 x^2\,dx = \frac{1}{3} \]

When this checklist is enough

Use this guide for homework, technical notes, and medium-size documents. For thesis-level formatting, add a final consistency pass after conversion.
